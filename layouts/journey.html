As far back as I can remember, I’ve always enjoyed creating — whether through painting, drawing, video, or sculpture. With a natural eye for design and strong problem-solving skills, I decided to pursue architecture as a way to combine both passions.

The foundation for my career in web development was a degree in Architecture, where I developed design thinking skills, problem-solving skills, and attention to detail. During my time across university and industry, I developed a passion for user-centric design and efficient solutions. My background allows me to approach technical challenges both creatively and analytically.

Although I initially enjoyed working in architecture, I soon realised that the reality of the field didn’t offer as much creativity or engaging problem-solving as I had hoped. Much of my work with CAD drawings and models felt like I was working on auto-pilot, working on very very repetitive work. Then one day I discovered AWWWARDS, a website showcasing some of the most incredible and interactive websites I have ever seen. I was inspired to reach the same heights. I thought this was a great opportunity to translate my passion for design and problem-solving into an exciting new career path. I initially dabbled in UX/UI design, but I wanted to take it further and actually build the things I imagined.


Coincidently, I discovered the opportunity to undertake a fully-funded Full-Stack Development Bootcamp, where I started my programming journey and gained proficiency in React and REST APIs. Since then, I’ve expanded my skills, learning additional libraries and languages such as Next.js, TypeScript, and SQL to create scalable, high-performance applications through personal projects like Exhibits and Quizmu. My experience in architecture has helped me design intuitive interfaces and create engaging user experiences, all built with a system focused on structure and modularity.
